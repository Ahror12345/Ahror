A new type of skateboard wheels were invented made out of polyrethane invented by Frank Nasworthy. He created a company called Cadillac Wheels. Before wheels were made out of metal or clay. The invention of Frank's wheel made skating popular again.

In the 1980s skatboarding comapnies were runned on skateboards. Their main focus was on vertical ramps. The invention of the ollie by Alan Gelfand in 1976 allowed for more tricks.Freestyle skating also remained popular throughout the period.

1990s-Present

Skateboarding during the 1990s was mainly dominated by street skating. Most board sizes were about 7.25 to 8 inches. The wheels made out of hard polyurethane. Wheel sizes were small making the board lighter which meant tricks were done easily.

Skateboarding got so popular that even people under the age of 18 started.Skateboarding and skateparks began to pop up through out the country. According to the American Sports Data skating increased by 60 percent between 1999 and 2002 going from 7.8 million to 12.5 million skaters.
